ABOUT

Illinois, the Prairie State, became the 21st state to join the United States of America on December 3rd, 1818. Illinois is a state located in the Midwestern region of the United States. It is the sixth most populous state in the country with a population of approximately 12.7 million people. The state capital is Springfield, while the largest city is Chicago, which is also one of the largest cities in the US. Illinois has a diverse economy, with industries ranging from agriculture to manufacturing, and is known for being a major transportation hub due to its central location. The state is home to several Fortune 500 companies, including Boeing, Caterpillar, and McDonald's, among others. Illinois is bordered by Wisconsin to the north, Indiana to the east, Missouri to the west, and Kentucky to the south. Lake Michigan, one of the Great Lakes, borders the northeastern part of the state, and the Mississippi River forms its western border. The state is also known for its rich cultural and historical heritage. It played a significant role in the development of jazz and blues music and is home to several famous landmarks and museums, including the Willis Tower (formerly known as the Sears Tower), the Art Institute of Chicago, and the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum. In terms of climate, Illinois experiences four distinct seasons, with cold winters and hot summers. The state has a diverse landscape, ranging from flat plains in the north and central regions to rolling hills and forests in the south.
